# AI Career Builder (for Startup Applicants)


This skill is for job seekers targeting **people-facing AI roles at startups** (under 150 people). You are not an engineer. You are a trainer, consultant, coach, or strategic generalist who happens to deeply understand AI because you've shipped 40+ systems. Your differentiator: you can both build and get people to adopt new ways of working.

**Employment model:** You operate through your US LLC (Alex Myers Consulting LLC) as an independent contractor. You cannot accept W2 employment. Contract and retainer engagements are preferred. Full-time FTE is acceptable only if the company can pay your LLC. You also maintain existing training and coaching clients, so engagements must allow schedule flexibility.

**Core premise:** Startups hire people who can make AI stick. Your GitHub is proof you understand the tech; your change management cert and consulting track record prove you can get teams to use it. Every application, interview answer, and portfolio piece should either surface a shipped system that automated a manual process or describe how you got a team to adopt new workflows.

**Target roles:** AI Adoption Consultant, AI Enablement Specialist, AI Change Management Consultant, Chief of Staff, Head of AI Adoption, AI People Operations, Strategic Generalist, AI Trainer, AI Workshop Lead, Executive Operations + AI Enablement. Contract, retainer, or FTE (paid to LLC).

## Leading words

- **Bolt**: a short, tight, decisive piece of copy that does one job (headline, hook, close) and stops. Bolts land. Long paragraphs drift.
- **Bridge**: a transition that connects what you *do* to what they *need*. Every resume bullet, cover letter, and interview answer is a bridge or it's noise.
- **Evidence line**: a single sentence carrying a specific, verifiable result (shipped system, metric, pull request, product launch) that does the convincing alone.
- **GitHub proof**: a specific public repo on github.com/armchairfuturist-code that demonstrates the capability you're selling. A shipping repository is worth more than a testimonial.

3. **Bridge bolt**: `[What you bring to this specific team]`
   - "Looking to bring this build-ship-adopt rhythm to [Company]'s [team/product]. I can both understand the tech and get people to use it."

### Step 3: Resume bullet formula

Every bullet point follows this structure:

> **\[Action verb\] \[what you did with people\] \[result/metric\]**

This is a people-facing role. Lead with adoption, training, and consulting wins. GitHub repos are supporting evidence, not the headline.

Examples:
- **Led AI adoption** across 40+ production systems for clients on 4 continents: trained teams, built workflows, got people to change how they work.
- **Delivered AI workshops** that got [X] teams to adopt automated workflows, cutting manual processes by [X]%.
- **Built autonomous AI pipelines** at github.com/armchairfuturist-code/[repo] that automated [capability]: then trained teams to maintain and extend them.

**Rules:**
- Every bullet must *either* have a number *or* a GitHub link
- No bullet without a tangible outcome
- Cut any bullet that a generic applicant could write
- People-facing work gets the metric; technical work gets the GitHub link as supporting proof

## Interview Prep

### Step 1: Technical screen prep

This is a people-facing role. The screen is about AI knowledge and adoption ability, not coding.

| Topic | How to prepare |
|---|---|
| **AI knowledge** | Reference your 40+ shipped systems. "I've built [type of system] for [client]. I know how it works, and I know how teams adopt it." |
| **Change management** | Your cert + experience. "I'm change management certified. I've led adoption initiatives that got teams to change how they work." |
| **Training / enablement** | "I've delivered workshops on [topic]. I can explain AI concepts to non-technical stakeholders." |
| **Product sense** | "I shipped [system] for [client type] which solved [problem]. Here's how I thought about the adoption challenge." |

**Key rule:** Never answer a question without connecting it to team adoption. "I've done exactly this, and here's how I got the team to use it."

### Step 2: Behavioral answers (STAR → STAR+G)

Standard STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) with **G for GitHub** as supporting proof:

- **Situation:** What was the problem or context?
- **Task:** What was your specific responsibility?
- **Action:** What did you build, consult on, or train teams to use?
- **Result:** What happened? (metric, adoption win, client outcome)
- **GitHub:** "You can see the technical implementation at github.com/armchairfuturist-code/[repo]." (optional, supporting)

Example:
> "A client was spending 15 hours/week on manual lead follow-up (S). I owned the solution design and team adoption (T). I built an AI conversation agent and trained their team to maintain it (A). It cut their response time from 4 hours to 90 seconds and recovered ~10 hours/week (R). The architecture is public at github.com/armchairfuturist-code/[project] if you want to see the technical side (G)."

### Step 3: Take-home project strategy

If the company sends a take-home:
1. **Treat it like an open-source contribution**: clean code, descriptive commits, README with architecture decisions
2. **Push it to a repo** on your GitHub (private or public as they specify)
3. **Include a README** with:
   - What it does
   - Architecture decisions and trade-offs
   - How to run it
   - What you'd add with more time
4. **Mention it in your follow-up**: "I treated the take-home like a real project. You can see the commit history at [repo]."

**Completion criterion:** candidate ready for each interview stage with at least one specific GitHub repo reference per question area.

## Job Search Strategy & Offer Negotiation
Load `profile-data.md` for the user's target roles, employment model, and key metrics.

### Step 1: Build your target list

| Tier | Type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Core** | Stage, sector, and role that match your profile | Seed/Series A AI startups, 10-150 people, need adoption help |
| **Stretch** | Slightly above your apparent ceiling | Series B, Head of AI Adoption, Chief of Staff |
| **Moat** | Companies where your AI expertise + people skills are directly relevant | Building AI products but struggling with team adoption |

For each tier, identify 3-5 companies. Research:
- Who's the hiring manager or head of people?
- What's their product and who are their customers?
- Where does your adoption/enablement work overlap with their challenges?

### Step 2: Application outreach

**Warm intro** (preferred):
> "I'm a fan of [Company]'s work on [product]. I've helped teams across 4 continents adopt AI workflows and I'd love to hear how you're approaching adoption at [Company]. 10 minutes for a conversation? No ask, just curious."

**Cold application** (via email or portal):
- Cover letter per the template above
- Lead with team adoption wins, not GitHub
- Subject line: "Applicant for [Role]: 40+ AI systems shipped, teams adopted across 4 continents"

### Step 3: Offer evaluation

| Factor | What to look for |
|---|---|
| **Rate / compensation** | Market rate for role + stage. For contract: $100-200/hr or $8-15K/month retainer is typical for senior AI consultants. Check Glassdoor, Blind, comparable LLC rates. |
| **Equity** | For contract: ask for advisor shares or phantom equity. For FTE-to-LLC: negotiate consulting rate that compensates for lack of benefits. |
| **Payment model** | Must pay Alex Myers Consulting LLC. Invoicing terms: Net 15 or Net 30. No W2, no 1099 to you personally. |
| **Role scope** | Is the title aligned with your growth path? What does "level set" mean in 6 months? |
| **Schedule fit** | Can you maintain existing training/coaching clients? Ask about expected hours/week, async expectations, meeting cadence. |
| **Team** | Who's your manager? Do they ship? How's the team's GitHub? |
| **Product fit** | Can you use your AI skills on day one? |

### Step 4: Negotiation principles

| They say (objection) | You say (rephrase) |
|---|---|
| "That's our final offer" | "I understand. Is there flex on equity, advisor shares, or a longer contract term instead?" |
| "We can't go higher on rate" | "What about a 6-month retainer with a built-in rate review? Or a minimum monthly commitment regardless of hours?" |
| "We need a W2 employee" | "I operate through my LLC. I can deliver the same work, just paid to Alex Myers Consulting LLC. Most of my clients prefer this: no benefits overhead for you, cleaner for me." |
| "We need a decision by Friday" | "I can make a decision by [2-3 days after Friday]. I want to be sure this is right for both of us." |
| "What other offers do you have?" | "I'm in process with a few companies. I'm also maintaining my training practice. For now, I'm focused on whether this is the right fit." |

**Rules:**
- Never bluff about another offer
- Always ask for one more thing after the first "yes". The first concession is often not the last.
- If they can't move on rate, negotiate: contract length, minimum hours, start date, scope flexibility, advisor equity
- For LLC payment: most tech startups (especially under 150 people) are amenable. Frame it as a benefit: no payroll taxes, no benefits, no HR overhead for them

**Completion criterion:** at least one offer received with at least one negotiated term (comp, title, or flexibility).

### Writing Hygiene (anti-slop rules)

Every output line follows these constraints. The models in bio-builder.md, proposal-templates.md, and resume examples demonstrate the correct shape.

**No em-dashes.** Absolutely NO literal em-dashes (—) or pandoc/markdown variants. Use standard hyphens (-) or colons (:) exclusively.
**No contrastive negation.** "Not X, Y" is AI slop. State Y directly. Exception: explicit comparison that clarifies a genuine distinction.
**No empty adverbs.** "especially", "really", "actually", "simply", "essentially", "truly", "very", "highly", "critically". Every word carries weight or leaves.
**No filler hedging.** "quite", "somewhat", "rather", "fairly", "a bit". State the degree or don't.
**No intensifiers.** "extremely", "incredibly", "remarkably", "absolutely". The claim stands or falls on evidence.
**No passive where active works.** "The repo was built" -> "I built the repo."
**One sentence, one claim.** No commas stacking three ideas.

**Check:** read every line back. A line that could open a generic LinkedIn post gets cut.
