# Supplement interaction reference

Common interaction patterns for longevity-optimisation stacks. Covers absorption competition, synergy, antagonism, and circadian timing. Use these tables when evaluating a user's stack or proposing new interventions.

---

## Absorption competition

Minerals and other divalent cations compete for the same transporters. When two or more are present at high doses, absorption of each is reduced unless doses are separated.

| Pair | Mechanism | Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Zinc ↔ Copper | Share enterocyte transporters (MT, DMT1, ZIP4). High zinc upregulates metallothionein which traps copper in enterocytes. | **Always pair** when zinc >30 mg/day. Typical ratio: 15–30:1 Zn:Cu (e.g. 30 mg Zn + 1–2 mg Cu). |
| Calcium ↔ Iron | Calcium inhibits both heme and non-heme iron absorption via DMT1 competition. | **Separate by 4+ hours.** Don't take calcium with iron-rich meals or iron supplements. |
| Calcium ↔ Magnesium | Compete for common absorption pathways at high doses. | **Take at different meals** if both are high dose (>200 mg each). Magnesium at night, calcium with a meal. |
| Iron ↔ Zinc | DMT1 competition, especially on empty stomach. | **Separate by 2+ hours.** Take iron with vitamin C, zinc with food. |
| Iron ↔ Copper | Copper is required for iron mobilisation (hephaestin, ceruloplasmin). Low copper can cause functional iron deficiency even with normal ferritin. | If ferritin is high but MCV is low, check copper status — don't just add iron. |
| Magnesium ↔ High-dose Zinc | Zinc can reduce Mg absorption at very high doses (>50 mg/day). | **Separate by 2+ hours.** Rare at typical doses — flag only if both are near ceiling. |

### Fat-soluble vitamins

A, D, E, and K require dietary fat for absorption. Take with the fattiest meal of the day. K2 (menaquinone-7) specifically — fat-soluble and also needs Mg for activation.

Vitamin D upregulates intestinal calcium absorption — if adding high-dose D (5000+ IU), ensure Mg status is adequate or risk functional Mg deficiency.

## Synergy pairs (beneficial combinations)

| Pair | Mechanism | Clinical value |
|---|---|---|
| **B12 + methylfolate + TMG** | Methylfolate feeds methyl groups to MTR (B12-dependent); TMG provides alternative BHMT pathway. Two parallel routes for homocysteine clearance. | **Core methylation stack.** All three needed for optimal homocysteine reduction. Missing one leaves a pathway uncovered. |
| **NAC + glycine** | NAC supplies cysteine (rate-limiting GSH precursor). Glycine is the second substrate. Without glycine, GSH synthesis is capped at ~50% of max. | **Take together.** NAC alone without glycine is suboptimal. Best taken 60–90 min before bed. |
| **Vitamin D + magnesium + K2** | Mg is required for D hydroxylation (CYP2R1, CYP27B1). K2 directs calcium into bone instead of soft tissue. | **Essential triad.** D without Mg can deplete Mg. D without K2 may increase arterial calcification risk. |
| **Curcumin + piperine** | Piperine inhibits glucuronidation, increasing curcumin bioavailability ~2000%. | **Required co-administration** for meaningful systemic levels. Use standardised product (Longvida, Meriva) which partially solves this. |
| **Iron + vitamin C** | Vitamin C reduces Fe³⁺ to Fe²⁺ and chelates iron, enhancing absorption 2–6×. | **Take together** on empty stomach. Avoid calcium-rich foods/coffee within 1 hour. |
| **Creatine + beta-alanine** | Creatine supports phosphocreatine system (short bursts). Beta-alanine buffers H⁺ via carnosine (repeated efforts). Non-competing, complementary energy systems. | **Synergistic for performance.** No timing constraints. |
| **Zinc + magnesium (ZMA style)** | Zinc supports testosterone synthesis. Magnesium supports sleep and recovery. Both are commonly low. | **Effective taken before bed** for sleep quality and recovery, provided copper status is maintained. |
| **Glycine + magnesium glycinate** | Magnesium glycinate already provides glycine; additional glycine amplifies sleep/GABA benefit. | **Standard night stack.** Mg glycinate as base + 3–10 g glycine for sleep onset. |
| **DHEA + retatrutide / GLP-1 agonists** | Rapid fat loss suppresses HPT axis. DHEA supports androgen production during caloric deficit. | **Monitor free T** monthly during rapid fat loss. DHEA is a support, not a replacement. |
| **CoQ10 (ubiquinol) + PQQ** | PQQ stimulates mitochondrial biogenesis; CoQ10 supports electron transport chain function. | **Supportive pair for mitochondrial health.** PQQ works better with exercise. |

## Antagonism / caution pairs

| Pair | Risk | Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| **High-dose zinc (>40 mg/day, long-term)** | Copper depletion → anaemia, neutropenia, neurodegeneration risk | Pair 1–2 mg Cu per 15–30 mg Zn. Test copper/ceruloplasmin every 3 months. |
| **High-dose calcium + iron** | Calcium blocks heme and non-heme iron absorption, worsening deficiency | Separate by 4+ hours. Prioritise iron over calcium in deficient users. |
| **Vitamin E + vitamin K (high dose)** | Both fat-soluble; compete for absorption and transport at high pharmacological doses | Only relevant at supraphysiological doses (>400 IU E + >5 mg K). Standard supplement doses are fine. |
| **5-HTP / St. John's Wort + serotonergic compounds** | Serotonin syndrome risk (rare but serious with MAOIs, SSRIs, or multiple serotonergic agents) | Flag strongly if user reports taking antidepressants. Do not stack multiple serotonergic supplements. |
| **GABAergics (multiple)** | Excessive sedation, respiratory depression risk with alcohol, benzodiazepines, or multiple sedating supplements | Warn if user combines: magnesium, glycine, theanine, melatonin, ashwagandha, lemon balm, GABA itself. |
| **High-dose iodine + selenium deficiency** | Iodine upregulates thyroid activity; without sufficient selenium, peroxidases can't process it → thyroid tissue damage | Ensure selenium >80 µg/day before any iodine loading protocol. |
| **Ashwagandha + thyroid medication** | Ashwagandha can increase T4 conversion to T3. May amplify levothyroxine effect. | Monitor TSH 6–8 weeks after starting ashwagandha if on thyroid meds. |

## Timing / circadian guidance

### Morning (first 2 hours after waking)

| Compound | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Thyroid (T4/T3) | Empty stomach, 30–60 min before food/coffee. Calcium/iron/coffee block absorption. |
| Iron | With vitamin C, away from coffee/tea/calcium. Empty stomach preferred. |
| Adaptogens (rhodiola, ashwagandha) | Rhodiola is stimulating — morning or early afternoon. Ashwagandha can be morning or night. |
| B-complex | Mildly activating for some — take early to avoid sleep disruption. |

### Midday / with largest meal

| Compound | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Vitamin D + K2 + Mg | With fattiest meal for absorption. Mg helps D metabolism. |
| Curcumin | With meal + fat + piperine. |
| CoQ10 / ubiquinol | Fat-soluble, with meal. |
| Minerals (Zn, Cu, Ca) | With food to reduce GI irritation. Separate competing pairs across meals. |
| Omega-3s (fish oil) | With meal to improve absorption and reduce burping. |

### Evening / before bed

| Compound | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Magnesium glycinate | GABAergic, promotes sleep onset. |
| Glycine | Lowers core body temperature, promotes sleep. |
| Zinc | Supports sleep quality, testosterone (T production peaks during sleep). |
| Theanine | Calming, synergises with Mg glycinate. |
| NAC + glycine (night stack) | GSH synthesis peaks during sleep. Take 60–90 min before bed. |
| Melatonin | Low dose (0.3–1 mg) only. Higher doses can blunt natural production and cause morning grogginess. |
| Apigenin | GABAergic, promotes sleep maintenance. |
| GABA (oral) | May support sleep onset; limited BBB penetration. |

### Avoid these at night

- Caffeine (half-life 5–6h, 8h+ for slow metabolisers — CYP1A2 SNP)
- B-complex (activating for many)
- Rhodiola (stimulating for some)
- L-tyrosine (stimulating, dopamine precursor)
- Modafinil, semax, noopept, phenylpiracetam (directly activating)

## Dose scheduling / cycling guidance

| Compound | Cycle | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Creatine | 3–5 g/day, no cycling needed | Saturation after 3–4 weeks of loading; maintenance steady-state. |
| Beta-alanine | 3–6 g/day, no cycling needed | 2–4 weeks to saturate carnosine. Tingle (paresthesia) is harmless. |
| Curcumin | 4 weeks on / 1 week off | Resensitisation of detox pathways. |
| NAC | 3 months continuous, then 2-week break | Theoretical concern about long-term antioxidant interference with exercise adaptations. |
| Ashwagandha | 8–12 weeks, then 2–4 week break | Tolerance and potential thyroid overstimulation with extended use. |
| Rhodiola | 4–8 weeks, then 1–2 week break | Maintains sensitivity to adaptogenic effect. |
| Melatonin | 0.3–1 mg, 5 days on / 2 off or as needed | Prevent tolerance and blunting of natural production. |
| BPC-157 | 4–6 weeks on, 2–4 off | Standard peptide cycling. |
| TB-500 | 4–6 weeks on, 4–6 off | Accumulates in tissues — longer off period needed. |
| SS-31 | 4–8 weeks on, 4 off | Mitochondrial peptide cycling. |
| Sermorelin | 3–6 months on, 1–3 months off | Maintain pituitary sensitivity to GHRH. |
| Modafinil | 1–3×/week, not daily | Rapid tolerance with daily use. |
| Phenylpiracetam | 1–2×/week max | Tolerance develops within 3–4 days of daily use. |
